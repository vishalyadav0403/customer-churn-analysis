# Customer Churn Analysis — Predictive Modeling & Retention Insights

## Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end customer churn analytics pipeline built using Python, MySQL, SQL, Machine Learning, and Power BI. The objective was to identify customer churn drivers, predict high-risk customers, and generate actionable retention insights using a telecom customer dataset containing 7,000+ records.

The project combines:
- data cleaning & validation,
- exploratory data analysis (EDA),
- predictive modeling,
- SQL-driven business analysis,
- and interactive dashboard visualization.

---

# Business Problem

Customer churn significantly impacts recurring revenue and customer lifetime value in subscription-based businesses. Understanding why customers leave and identifying high-risk segments early can help businesses improve retention strategies and reduce revenue loss.

This project aims to:
- analyze customer behavior patterns,
- identify key churn indicators,
- classify customers by churn risk,
- and provide business-focused retention recommendations.

---

# Objectives

- Perform structured data cleaning and validation
- Identify churn patterns using exploratory analysis
- Build predictive churn classification models
- Segment customers into churn risk tiers
- Develop SQL-based business analysis workflows
- Create interactive Power BI dashboards for stakeholder reporting
- Generate retention strategy recommendations

# Data Cleaning & Validation

Key preprocessing activities included:
- handling missing values,
- datatype correction,
- duplicate validation,
- leakage detection,
- anomaly identification,
- structured consistency checks.

Leakage variables such as:
- `Churn Reason`
- `Churn Score`
- `Churn Label`

were excluded from predictive modeling to prevent target leakage and unrealistic model performance.

---

# Exploratory Data Analysis

EDA was conducted to identify:
- churn behavior patterns,
- high-risk customer segments,
- pricing sensitivity,
- tenure-related churn trends,
- and service-level risk factors.

### Key Findings
- Month-to-month contract customers showed the highest churn rates
- Short-tenure customers were significantly more likely to churn
- Fiber optic internet users exhibited elevated churn behavior
- Electronic check payment users demonstrated higher churn exposure
- Long-term contracts correlated with stronger customer retention

---

# Predictive Modeling

Machine learning models were developed to classify customer churn risk.

## Models Used
- Logistic Regression
- Random Forest Classifier

## Modeling Workflow
- feature encoding,
- train-test split,
- classification evaluation,
- feature importance analysis,
- probability-based risk segmentation.

## Evaluation Metrics
- Accuracy
- Precision
- Recall
- F1 Score
- ROC-AUC

---

# Risk Segmentation

Customers were segmented into:
- High Risk
- Medium Risk
- Low Risk

based on churn probability scores generated by the predictive model.

This segmentation enables:
- targeted retention campaigns,
- proactive customer engagement,
- and revenue risk monitoring.

# Key Business Insights

- Customers with month-to-month contracts represent the highest churn-risk segment
- Short customer tenure strongly correlates with churn probability
- Higher monthly charges increase churn exposure
- Fiber optic service users demonstrate elevated churn behavior
- Electronic check payment customers show higher churn rates
- Long-term contracts contribute significantly to customer retention stability

---

# Business Recommendations

## Retention Strategies
- Introduce loyalty incentives for month-to-month customers
- Improve onboarding programs for early-tenure customers
- Optimize pricing plans for high-charge customer segments
- Develop targeted retention campaigns for high-risk customers
- Monitor high-risk service combinations proactively
